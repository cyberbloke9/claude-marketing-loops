# Hook Bank v1

Hooks mapped pain point → psychological mechanism → template → example. Rules: ≤3s spoken / ≤10 words on a slide; must name a specific (locality, number, date); direct-to-camera for video. Winners and losers get logged by `/loop-measure`; refresh monthly via `/loop-persona`.

Legend: **[P1]** Upgrader · **[P2]** Investor · pain codes: PRICE (81% anxiety), DELIVERY (98% completion), QUALITY (93%), YIELD, EXIT, RERA.

## Loss aversion

1. **[P1·PRICE]** "Waiting 6 months cost Whitefield buyers ₹__L. Here's the math." — *the cost of inaction, quantified*
2. **[P1·DELIVERY]** "This ₹1.2Cr flat may never be delivered. The warning was public." — *avoidable loss + it was checkable*
3. **[P2·EXIT]** "You can buy in __ in a week. Selling takes __ months." — *liquidity trap*
4. **[P1·PRICE]** "The discount ends when the tower does. Literally." — *scarcity tied to construction stage*

## Curiosity gap (must be resolved by slide 5–6 / mid-video)

5. **[P1·DELIVERY]** "3 of this builder's last 4 projects were late. The 4th is the interesting one."
6. **[P2·YIELD]** "The locality with Bengaluru's best rental yield isn't Koramangala. It's not even close."
7. **[P1·QUALITY]** "The sample flat is lying to you in 3 places."
8. **[P2·RERA]** "One line in the RERA filing tells you if a project will be late."

## Social proof / consensus

9. **[P2·YIELD]** "63% of Indians now pick real estate over stocks. The data says they're __ right." — *ANAROCK, cite on-slide*
10. **[P1·PRICE]** "71% of buyers delayed this year. Here's what the 21% who didn't know."
11. **[P1·DELIVERY]** "98% of buyers demand delivery guarantees. Almost none check the one document that predicts it."

## Contrarian / myth-break (Candid Analyst signature)

12. **[P2·YIELD]** "Your broker's 'appreciation' number is a marketing figure. Here's the registered-price truth."
13. **[P1·PRICE]** "Per-sq-ft price is the most misleading number in Indian real estate."
14. **[P2·YIELD]** "Gold beat your flat this decade — in 4 of India's 7 big cities."
15. **[P1·QUALITY]** "Amenities are a depreciating asset. Location data isn't."

## Specificity / receipt-showing

16. **[P1·PRICE]** "Sarjapur Road, registered prices, last 90 days. Not asking prices — registered."
17. **[P2·EXIT]** "Every resale in __ this quarter, on one chart."
18. **[P1·DELIVERY]** "We tracked 40 Bengaluru builders' delivery records. Screenshot this list."

## Divergence (city ≠ market) — NEW

21. **[P1·PRICE / P2·YIELD]** "<Premium locality> got cheaper this quarter. <Budget locality> rose __%." — *same-city divergence; the city average is a lie. First used: 2026-07-03 Weekly Ledger (Jubilee Hills / Medchal).* NEW

## Authority-conflict (who do you believe) — NEW

22. **[P2·EXIT/PRICE]** "<Source A> says <+X%>. <Source B> says <+Y%>. Same city, same quarter." — *two authorities contradicting each other; TERREM resolves. First used: 2026-07-09 Ledger #2 (ANAROCK/PropEquity, VS-CONTRAST cover).* NEW

## Challenge / utility CTA

19. **[P1·all]** "Check your builder in 2 minutes. If this chart is red, walk."
20. **[P2·YIELD]** "Run your locality through this before your next site visit."

## Format notes

- Reactive lane: hooks 12/13-style contrarian takes react to news within 48h ("RBI just cut rates. Here's who actually benefits — it's not you.")
- Every hook's asset must end on a *checkable action* on intel.terrem.in — hook opens the gap, TERREM closes it. That's the flywheel.
- Log per-hook 3s-hold and swipe-through in `metrics/` — retire the bottom third monthly.
