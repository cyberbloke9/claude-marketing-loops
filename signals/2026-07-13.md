## Signal 1: NCR just crossed the line Knight Frank itself calls "unaffordable"

- **What the data shows:** Knight Frank India's Affordability Index (H1 2026) shows NCR's EMI-to-income ratio has worsened to 65% — far past the 50% threshold Knight Frank defines as the affordability ceiling — and MMR also sits above that 50% line. Six of the other eight tracked cities remain within the affordable band: Ahmedabad 23% (most affordable), Kolkata 25%, Pune 28%, Chennai 29%, Bengaluru 35% (worsened marginally YoY), Hyderabad 41%. Knight Frank attributes the cushion in the affordable six to the RBI's cumulative 125bps of rate cuts through 2025 — a tailwind NCR and MMR buyers aren't getting relief from because price growth is outrunning it. Source: [Business Standard](https://www.business-standard.com/industry/news/home-affordability-remains-strong-in-6-of-8-cities-knight-frank-126070300593_1.html), citing Knight Frank India, published 3 Jul 2026.
- **Why now:** freshest India-wide affordability benchmark of the year, released this week; gives NCR's price story (covered generically last loop via ANAROCK Q2 supply data) a second, independent metric — this one says "unaffordable by the analyst's own definition," not just "prices rose."
- **Exclusivity:** LOW (public index) — TERREM's synthesis is translating a ratio into a rupee reality: what does a 65% EMI-to-income ratio actually cost a NCR household monthly, and how that compares to the 50%/35%/28% bands other buyers live in.

## Signal 2: Homes under ₹50L are disappearing from the market — not getting cheaper, disappearing

- **What the data shows:** Knight Frank H1 2026: sales of homes priced below ₹50 lakh fell 15% YoY to 32,063 units (from 37,796 a year earlier) across the top 8 cities, now just 19% of total sales. The ₹50L–₹1Cr mid-segment dipped 5% YoY to 46,490 units (27% share). Meanwhile homes priced ₹1Cr+ now account for 54% of all sales, up from 49% last year — total sales rose a mere 0.7% to 1.71 lakh units, meaning the entire market's "growth" is concentrated at the top while the bottom two-thirds of the price ladder shrinks in absolute volume. Sources: [The Hans India](https://www.thehansindia.com/business/luxury-demand-keeps-housing-sales-afloat-affordable-segment-slips-1096298), [Newkerala](https://www.newkerala.com/news/a/residential-sales-key-indian-cities-cross-171-lakh-138.htm), citing Knight Frank India H1 2026, published ~9 Jul 2026.
- **Why now:** freshest hard registered-sales confirmation of the premiumization trend that `personas/personas.md` already flags from ANAROCK's H1 2025 survey (affordable <₹45L demand "collapsed to 17%") — this is a full year later, with transaction data instead of sentiment data, showing the squeeze has deepened, not stabilized.
- **Exclusivity:** LOW (public report) — TERREM's angle: this isn't affordable housing getting cheaper or buyers "waiting it out," it's affordable *supply* vanishing from developer pipelines, which is a structural, not cyclical, story.
- **Persona × pain:** P1×PRICE
- **Suggested hook direction:** #14 family (contrarian myth-break) — "63% of Indians pick real estate as their #1 asset. This year, the homes most of them can actually afford dropped 15% in number sold. The market isn't waiting for you — it's leaving your price bracket."
