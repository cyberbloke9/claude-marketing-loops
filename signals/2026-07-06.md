## Signal 4: Chennai builders can no longer advertise a fake completion date — as of this week

- **What the data shows:** TNRERA issued a circular dated 24 Jun 2026, effective 1 Jul 2026, introducing a structured penalty framework (₹50,000–₹5 lakh) for non-compliant real estate advertisements. False advertising — misleading claims on completion dates, amenities, or carpet area — draws up to ₹5 lakh per instance. Every ad (print, digital, TV, hoardings, social media) must now display the project's TNRERA registration number, a scannable Form-C QR code, the authority's website, accurate location, and promoter contact details. Sources: [Storyboard18](https://www.storyboard18.com/how-it-works/tnrera-cracks-down-on-misleading-real-estate-ads-with-tough-new-guidelines-73314.htm), [Verified.RealEstate](https://community.verified.realestate/article/tnrera-puts-a-price-on-non-compliant-property-ads-new-circular-introduces-penalties-up-to-%E2%82%B95-lakh/), circular effective 1 Jul 2026.

## Signal 5: A homebuyer group just won the right to bankrupt their builder — even after filing with RERA

- **What the data shows:** On 8 Jun 2026, NCLT New Delhi admitted a Section 7 insolvency petition (Case No. CP (IB)–182(ND)/2024) filed by 176 allottees (99 units) of Raheja Developers' "Revanta" project in Sector 78, Gurugram — a 18.72-acre tower development that missed its extended possession deadline of Jul 2022, with buyers having paid 90–95% of the sale consideration. The bench (Justice Anupinder Singh Grewal, Ravindra Chaturvedi) held that homebuyers' payments constitute "financial debt" under Section 5(8)(f) of the IBC, and — critically — that a pending RERA or consumer-forum case is NOT a bar to also pursuing insolvency proceedings against the builder. CIRP was ordered project-specific (not company-wide), per the NCLAT's Vatika precedent. Sources: [LiveLawBiz](https://www.livelawbiz.com/amp/ibc/nclt-delhi-admits-cirp-against-revanta-project-of-raheja-developers-holds-pendency-of-hrera-or-consumer-proceedings-no-bar-to-ibc-remedies-538243), [TaxScan](https://www.taxscan.in/top-stories/infrastructure-delays-and-rera-proceedings-cannot-defeat-homebuyers-insolvency-plea-nclt-1448542) (Case: 2026 TAXSCAN (NCLT) 189), judgment dated 8 Jun 2026.
- **Why now:** a genuine legal-precedent story — most delayed-possession buyers don't know insolvency is a parallel track to RERA, not a substitute for it; CREDAI puts ~450 real-estate projects nationally under IBC proceedings, so the applicability is broad, not a one-off.
- **Exclusivity:** LOW (public judgment) — TERREM's enrichment: pair with `builder_health_warnings` once NCR coverage lands, to flag which stalled projects already qualify for this route.
- **Persona × pain:** P1×DELIVERY/RERA
- **Suggested hook direction:** #2 family (loss aversion + it was checkable) — "Filed a RERA complaint against your builder and still waiting? A Gurugram buyer group just proved that doesn't stop you from also bankrupting them. Here's the precedent."
