TNRERA's new advertising circular took effect on 1 July 2026. It puts a price on lies: false claims about completion dates, carpet area, or amenities now draw penalties of ₹50,000 to ₹5 lakh per instance, and every real-estate ad — print, digital, hoarding, or social — must display the project's TNRERA registration number and a scannable Form-C QR code.

The QR is the part that matters to a buyer. It turns a glossy hoarding into something you can verify in seconds instead of taking on trust.
