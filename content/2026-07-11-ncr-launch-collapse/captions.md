Delhi-NCR builders launched 40% fewer homes in Q2 2026 (11,205 units, down from 18,760) — the sharpest supply pullback of any top-7 city, with Noida–Greater Noida launches down 72%. Buyers didn't slow to match: sales dipped only 6%, to 13,365 units. The region sold more homes than it launched.

That gap is why NCR posted +13% average price growth, the highest of any big city this quarter. It isn't demand euphoria — it's a supply drought. For a buyer, the uncomfortable part: waiting for a dip assumes new launches will pressure prices. Here, 40% fewer did.
